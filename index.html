The Ocean

The ocean covers more than 70% of Earth's surface and plays a crucial role in regulating the planet's climate and supporting life. Scientists continue to explore the ocean to discover new species, understand ecosystems, and study geological formations beneath the sea.

Marine Life

From microscopic plankton to the enormous blue whale, marine life exists in an extraordinary range of forms. Coral reefs, kelp forests, and deep-sea vents all host unique communities of organisms adapted to their environments.

Deep Sea Exploration

  • Remotely operated vehicles (ROVs)
  • Deep-sea submersibles
  • Autonomous underwater drones
  • Advanced sonar mapping systems

Why It Matters

Studying the ocean helps scientists understand climate change, biodiversity, and natural resources. Many discoveries from ocean research contribute to medicine, environmental protection, and sustainable development.
